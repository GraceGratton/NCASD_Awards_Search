Award Journey
🔍 Discovery 1–3 years before application
  • Define your career goal by considering what you want to achieve and the impact you hope to make in your field
  • Plan your academic journey by considering what steps you need to take to reach your career goal
  • Explore awards that support your goals, even if you are not eligible yet
💡 Pro Tip!  In your application, be as specific about your career goal and the impact you want to make. Clear and precise goals make for a winning essay.
⚙️ Development 3–12 months before application
  • Learn about what makes a strong applicant by reviewing award criteria, past recipients, and selection priorities
  • Build your profile by developing the skills, experiences, and leadership qualities that align with the award’s goals
  • Seek mentorship, feedback, and opportunities to grow in areas that strengthen your future application
📋 Application During application
  • Make a detailed application plan with deadlines for writing essays and requesting letters of recommendation
  • Tell your story clearly by connecting your experiences, goals, and impact to the award’s mission
  • Seek feedback from our office, as well as from mentors, advisors, and peers to make your application as strong as possible
💡 Pro Tip!  Start your application early. Strong applications take time to craft and benefit from several rounds of feedback and revision.

Our Office
✓ What We Do
  • Provide assistance for supported awards
  • Review your applications in person and asynchronously
  • Clarify your academic and professional goals
  • Host scholar development workshops
✕ What We Don’t Do
  • We are not financial aid
  • Support awards outside of our listed awards
  • Resume review or interview practice (unless it’s for one of our awards)
  • Review essays with students who have not opted into an award
3 Ways to Work With Us
1
One-on-one Mentorship
Opt into our one-on-one mentorship program for award applications using our opt-in forms, found on ERNIE → Departments → Nationally Competitive Awards.
Note: To receive assistance on an award, you must opt-in.
2
Interest Meetings
In the spring semester, book an interest meeting with a peer mentor to explore the awards we support and create an application plan.
3
Professional Development Workshops & Award Presentations
Attend either one of our professional development workshops or in-depth award presentations, during spring semester only.
